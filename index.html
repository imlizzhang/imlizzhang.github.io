Research Interests

My research focuses on developing i-Tree Research Suite - HydroPlus to simulate the effects of changing urban surfaces and human activities on the hydrological cycle, and the benefits of trees on the environment. My main contributions are:

1) i-Tree Hydro - creating algorisms for the Green Infrastructures devices, allowing multiple GIs running in parallel and series in other spatial models like i-Tree Buffer.

2) i-Tree Cool Air – To generate a national map without abrupt temperature changes along the Thiessen Polygon boundaries, I employ an inverse-distance weighted method. This method allows us to obtain spatially weighted meteorological inputs from multiple weather stations. This work provides a more accurate representation of the temperature distribution across the entire map.

3) i-Tree Buffer - Developing a Stochastic Export Coefficient Model which can simulate annual variation in phosphorus loading from diffuse runoff to assist with nutrient management plans for river basins. Built the nutrient hotspot mapping water quality model i-Tree Buffer into HydroPlus, and allow this tool predicting shifts in hotspots over time. By considering both spatial and temporal dimensions, we can more accurately forecast changes in hotspots and their associated impacts.
